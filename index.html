01 The approach
Most ideas die from building the wrong thing well. I de-risk innovation by putting small, real products in front of users — fast — and letting their behaviour decide what to build next.
01

Start from the user, not the feature

Every project begins with a validated need — interviews, jobs-to-be-done, and the smallest thing that tests it.

02

Ship the MVP, learn in public

A live app in a week beats a perfect spec in a quarter. Real usage replaces opinion.

03

Pivot on evidence, not ego

From Vapporio's many pivots to a 4-month tea-market fit — I follow the data until the curve bends.

04

Industrialise what works

An engineer at heart: once there's fit, I hand a robust, manufacturable product to R&D.

03 The lean loop

How a hunch becomes fit.

The same loop runs under every app above — and under the products I lead at work. Tight cycles, real evidence, no wasted builds.

01

Build the smallest test

An MVP that isolates one risky assumption — the fastest honest version of the idea.

02

Measure real behaviour

Ship it to real users and watch what they do, not what they say. Signups, retention, the drop-offs.

03

Learn & decide

Double down, pivot, or kill it. Evidence decides the next loop — and each loop gets you closer to fit.
