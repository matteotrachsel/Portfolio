01 The approach
Most ideas die from building the wrong thing well. I de-risk innovation by putting small, real products in front of users — fast — and letting their behaviour decide what to build next.
01

Start from the user, not the feature

Every project begins with a validated need — interviews, jobs-to-be-done, and the smallest thing that tests it.

02

Ship the MVP, learn in public

A live app in a week beats a perfect spec in a quarter. Real usage replaces opinion.

03

Pivot on evidence, not ego

From Vapporio's many pivots to a 4-month tea-market fit — I follow the data until the curve bends.

04

Industrialise what works

An engineer at heart: once there's fit, I hand a robust, manufacturable product to R&D.

03 Product operating model

How I run product.

A repeatable loop ships one product. A modern operating model ships them again and again — empowered teams, pointed at outcomes, learning every week. This is how I structure product work, whether it's a weekend MVP or an organisation.

01

Empowered teams

Cross-functional teams own a problem, not a backlog. I hand teams outcomes to pursue and the autonomy to find the best solution — then get out of the way.

02

Outcomes over output

Success is a moved metric — activation, retention, revenue — not a list of features shipped. Roadmaps are bets on outcomes, not promises of scope.

03

Continuous discovery

Weekly contact with real users. Risky assumptions get tested with prototypes before a line of production code is written — discovery and delivery run in parallel.

04

Continuous delivery

Small, frequent releases. Shipping is a routine, not an event — so feedback loops stay tight, risk stays low, and the product keeps compounding.
